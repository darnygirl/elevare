## Stage 1 — Intake

**Assessment call (5 min → extend to 10 for Med given complexity):**

Diagnostic questions specific to Med:
1. Which exam + which country + timeline?
2. Current clinical role + years of experience?
3. Previous OET attempt? (score breakdown if yes)
4. Specific weakness they think they have? (speaking / writing / listening / reading)
5. Access to real English-speaking patients currently?

**Baseline diagnostic (15 min):**
- 3-min scripted read-aloud (clinical text) — assesses pronunciation + pacing
- Listen to a 2-min SBAR handover audio → re-tell in own words (listening + memory)
- Role-play: take patient history from a chest-pain presentation (you play patient, score against OET speaking rubric)
- Short write-up: summarise the role-play in 50 words (writing sample)

**Tier-match output:**
- Baseline OET skill scores (0-500 per skill, their current band)
- Gap to target (usually B = 350+ per skill)
- Recommended programme length (8 / 12 / 16 weeks)
- 3-week custom plan emailed within 24 hrs

## Stage 3 — Deliver (12-week curriculum)

### Week 1-2 — Foundations + fluency floor
- **Clinical vocabulary consolidation** — top 500 medical terms grouped by system
- **Pronunciation map** — target 30 commonly mispronounced clinical terms for the student's L1
- **OET speaking role-play framework** — the 5-phase structure (greet → history → explain → check → close)
- **Writing referral letter basics** — 1 template, 5 variations

**Milestone end of Week 2:** student can deliver one clean clinical handover without notes.

### Week 3-4 — History taking + patient-centred communication
- **SOCRATES / OLDCARTS frameworks** for symptom-taking
- **Empathy scripts** — 20 phrases students can deploy under pressure ("I can see this is difficult," "Let me make sure I understand")
- **Role-plays:** chest pain, shortness of breath, abdominal pain, headache, fall, stroke, diabetic crisis, post-op pain, mental health, paediatric
- **AI practice:** student does 3 role-plays/week with Elevare AI, coach reviews recordings

**Milestone end of Week 4:** full 5-min history-take scoring ≥ B in speaking (OET mock 1).

### Week 5-6 — Explaining procedures + consent
- **Explain-to-grandma framework** — medical jargon → plain English
- **Informed consent dialogue patterns**
- **10 common procedures to practice:** IV cannulation, NG tube, ECG, catheter, blood draw, injection, wound care, observations, pre-op, discharge
- **Writing:** patient leaflet draft (500 words)

**Milestone end of Week 6:** OET writing mock scoring ≥ B.

### Week 7-8 — Multidisciplinary team communication
- **SBAR / ISBAR handover mastery**
- **Telephone communication** — common scenarios (escalation, consultant call-back, pharmacy query)
- **Writing:** referral letters (5 types), discharge summaries
- **Complex role-play:** angry patient, grieving family, safeguarding disclosure, complaint handling

**Milestone end of Week 8:** OET listening + reading mocks scoring ≥ B.

### Week 9-10 — OET test strategy + practice
- **OET speaking paper: full mock timing + coaching**
- **OET writing paper: 2 full letters under time pressure**
- **OET reading Part A/B/C strategies**
- **OET listening strategies + practice**

**Milestone end of Week 10:** full OET mock 2 — target: all 4 skills at ≥ B.

### Week 11 — Weakness pass + confidence
- **Targeted drills** on the skill still below B
- **Test-day protocol coaching** — timing, nerves, recovery if you stumble
- **Rise layer intensified** — confidence work for test-week anxiety

### Week 12 — Final prep + graduation
- **OET mock 3 (full 4-skill)** — release score only if meets target
- **Test-week simulation** — day-before ritual, morning-of protocol
- **Graduation session** — cohort call, certificates, LinkedIn badge
- **Post-course support** — first 30 days free WhatsApp support through exam day
