## Positioning + ICP

**Primary ICP:** committed learners with a **deadline-driven reason** to lock in fluency gains in 12 weeks. Examples:
- "I'm getting married abroad in 6 months"
- "Job interview in 4 months where English will decide"
- "Visa requires IELTS 7.0 by Month 5"
- "Moving to English-speaking country in a quarter"
- "Child starts English-language school, I want to keep up"

**Secondary ICP:** ambitious learners plateauing on general fluency who want a finite, measurable 12-week push to break through.

**Who Fluency is NOT for:**
- Beginners (A0-A2) — they'd drown in the pace
- Specialist needs (medical, business) → Med or Pro
- Free-form maintenance learners → Speak

**Pricing:** $497 upfront for 12-week programme OR $199×3 monthly split. Raise to $697 upfront once 10+ testimonials land (target Month 3).

**Format:** 60-min weekly group call (6-max cohort) + 30-min weekly 1:1 + Elevare AI daily practice + structured curriculum + final assessment + graduation certificate + LinkedIn badge.

## Stage 1 — Intake

**Assessment call (10 min):**

Diagnostic questions:
1. What's your 12-week deadline / reason? (if vague, soft-suggest Speak instead)
2. Current level self-estimate + what you can prove (previous IELTS, etc.)
3. Time commitment you can realistically make (target: 45 min/day)
4. Can you commit to 12 weeks of cohort attendance?
5. What will "success" look like on Day 84 for you?

**Diagnostic (10 min):**
- 3-min speaking: describe your 12-week goal in as much detail as possible
- 5-min listening: listen to a 2-min native-speaker segment, summarize in your own words
- 2-min written: write 80-100 words on your 12-week plan

**Commitment conversation:**
- "Fluency is a 12-week minimum commitment. Can you put in 45 min/day? Miss no more than 2 sessions? Honestly?"
- If not → deflect to Speak
- If yes → cohort assignment

**Welcome plan (within 24 hrs):**
- 12-week roadmap PDF (preview of every week)
- Cohort start date (next cohort every 4 weeks)
- First homework: baseline self-assessment + 3 AI role-plays on target scenario

## Stage 3 — Deliver (12-week curriculum)

### Phase 1: Fluency floor (Weeks 1-2)

#### Week 1 — Baseline clearing + automaticity
**Objectives:**
- Clear hesitation markers ("uhh", "umm", excessive "like")
- Build automaticity on present-tense descriptions
- Introduce self + goals in 90 seconds without notes

**Session focus:**
- Hesitation awareness drill (students record themselves, count fillers)
- Rapid-fire Q&A: 50 questions in 10 min, fast responses over perfect responses
- 90-sec introduction rehearsal

**Homework:** 20-min daily AI practice on rapid-response scenarios + 1 recorded 90-sec intro submitted.

**Milestone:** Filler-rate dropped 50% from baseline.

#### Week 2 — Basic structures automatic
**Objectives:**
- Present simple + present continuous fully automatic
- Describe actions, routines, current situations without pause

**Session focus:**
- Pair interviews (cohort members interview each other on life routines)
- Describe-the-room exercises (visual grounding for present tense)

**Homework:** 15-min daily AI practice + describe your day in 3-min recording.

**Milestone:** 2-minute personal description delivered at consistent pace without major stumbles.

---

### Phase 2: Range + confidence (Weeks 3-4)

#### Week 3 — Past + future tense fluency
**Objectives:**
- Past tense under real-time speaking pressure
- Future forms (will / going to / present continuous for arrangements)
- Tell a 2-min story with clear time markers

**Session focus:**
- Story circle (each student tells 2-min story on prompt)
- Future-planning exercise: describe your next 6 months in detail

**Homework:** 3× AI story role-plays + 1× peer story-swap call.

**Milestone:** 2-min story with past-tense accuracy + 1-min future-plan description.

#### Week 4 — Opinion + comparison language
**Objectives:**
- Express opinions with nuance (hedging, softening, strengthening)
- Compare and contrast fluently
- Handle follow-up questions without freezing

**Session focus:**
- Structured debates (2v2 on cohort-relevant topics)
- Comparison drills (this vs that in 30 seconds)

**Homework:** 3× AI role-plays on opinion-giving + 1 recorded 3-min opinion piece on a topic you care about.

**Milestone:** Can argue a position, defend against one pushback, acknowledge the counter-point without conceding entirely.

**Checkpoint: Week 4 milestone assessment** (30 min 1:1 with coach, recorded for Week 12 comparison)

---

### Phase 3: Real-world deployment (Weeks 5-6)

#### Week 5 — Scenarios from student's actual life
**Objectives:**
- Apply all previous frameworks to student's real-world upcoming scenarios
- Build a personalized phrase bank for student's specific deadline goal (wedding / interview / move / etc.)

**Session focus:**
- Each cohort member presents their "most feared upcoming English scenario"
- Coach + peers role-play the scenario, students get multiple takes
- Build personalized phrase banks

**Homework:** 5× AI role-plays on personal scenario + 1× real-world "try it for real" attempt, reported back.

**Milestone:** Personalized phrase bank of 30 situation-specific phrases memorized.

#### Week 6 — High-stakes simulation
**Objectives:**
- Handle pressure (time, authority, unfamiliar interlocutor)
- Recovery from mistakes mid-conversation

**Session focus:**
- Timed scenarios (5-min deadlines on real-world simulations)
- "Devil's advocate" coach — asks hard questions, interrupts, challenges
- Recovery drill: coach deliberately misunderstands, students recover

**Homework:** 3× AI role-plays with "difficult counterpart" setting enabled + record + transcribe 1 session.

**Milestone:** Complete a 5-min high-pressure simulation without freezing.

---

### Phase 4: Precision + accuracy (Weeks 7-8)

#### Week 7 — Grammar pattern identification + targeted drills
**Objectives:**
- Identify student's 3-5 recurring grammar slippage patterns
- Target those patterns with focused drills

**Session focus:**
- Transcription review — each student brings a recording, circles slips, coach analyses patterns
- Pattern-specific drills (articles / third-person s / past perfect / preposition choice / whatever the cohort needs)

**Homework:** 15-min daily targeted drill on personal weakness + 1× recorded 3-min submission with self-assessment.

**Milestone:** 50% reduction in the top 3 identified grammar slippage patterns.

#### Week 8 — Register + tone matching
**Objectives:**
- Match register to context (formal / informal / professional / intimate)
- Signal authority or warmth deliberately through word choice

**Session focus:**
- Same message, 4 registers (peer / boss / stranger / elder)
- Tone drills — deliver the same content warmly, professionally, firmly, apologetically

**Homework:** Rewrite 5 real messages from your life in 2 different registers each + 3× AI role-plays in different registers.

**Milestone:** Can deliver one message in 4 different registers cleanly.

**Checkpoint: Week 8 milestone assessment** (30 min 1:1 with coach)

---

### Phase 5: Complex communication (Weeks 9-10)

#### Week 9 — Nuance + cultural English
**Objectives:**
- Handle sarcasm, understatement, implication
- Read between the lines of native-speaker English

**Session focus:**
- Native-speaker clip analysis (TV / podcast / movie) — identify implicit meaning
- Humour and irony exercises (safe practice environment)

**Homework:** Watch 2 native-speaker shows, submit 5 implications you caught (not just literal meaning).

**Milestone:** Can identify 3 instances of implicit meaning in a 5-min native-speaker clip.

#### Week 10 — Storytelling + persuasion
**Objectives:**
- Tell stories that land emotionally, not just transmit information
- Use persuasion techniques (repetition, triadic structures, rhetorical questions)

**Session focus:**
- Story workshop — each student tells their "Week 12 graduation story" (aspirational)
- Persuasion drills — convince the cohort of something (cohort votes on most persuasive)

**Homework:** Record a 3-min persuasive talk on a cause you care about + submit.

**Milestone:** Tell a 3-min emotional story with clear arc and one deliberate persuasion technique.

---

### Phase 6: Integration + final prep (Weeks 11-12)

#### Week 11 — Weak-spot pass + confidence
**Objectives:**
- Targeted work on the 1-2 skills still below target
- Build test-week / deadline-week confidence

**Session focus:**
- Personalized drill sessions (each student different based on Week 8 milestone gap)
- Confidence work (Rise layer intensified)
- Worst-case-scenario rehearsal — what if you freeze? What if they ask X? What if Y?

**Homework:** Tailored to each student's weakness + daily visualization / confidence routine.

**Milestone:** Personal weakness scored at target level.

#### Week 12 — Graduation + final assessment
**Objectives:**
- Final assessment across 4 skills vs Week 0 baseline
- Celebrate progress publicly
- Capture testimonial

**Session structure (Week 12):**
- Day 1: Final 30-min 1:1 assessment — recorded. Compared to baseline from Week 0.
- Day 3: Cohort graduation group call (90 min)
  - Each student tells a 3-min story: "What I can do now that I couldn't on Day 1"
  - Coach delivers certificate + LinkedIn badge + Rise summary for each
  - Cohort toast + celebration
- Day 5: Testimonial capture (on-camera preferred, written acceptable)
- Day 7: Post-course support begins (30 days of WhatsApp access to coach)

**Graduation criteria:**
- Measurable progress on Week 0 baseline across 4 skills
- If not hit → programme extends free per guarantee
- Student self-declaration of readiness for their original 12-week goal

## The measurable-progress rubric for the guarantee

Since Fluency promises "measurable progress in 12 weeks or we keep coaching," here's the rubric (specific, testable):

| Dimension | Week 0 baseline | Week 12 target |
|---|---|---|
| Speaking fluency (words/min sustained) | measured in 3-min speech | +20% minimum |
| Speaking accuracy (grammar slippage rate) | errors per 100 words | -40% minimum |
| Listening comprehension (native speaker at normal pace) | % correct on a 2-min segment Q&A | +25% absolute |
| Writing (rubric-scored 80-word sample) | baseline rubric score | +1 band minimum |
| Student-self-reported confidence (1-10) | baseline | +3 minimum |

**Hit 4 of 5 = graduated.** Hit fewer → free extension until hit.
