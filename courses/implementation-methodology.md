# Elevare Implementation Methodology — Universal Framework

> Applies to all 5 products: Elevare AI, Speak, Fluency, Pro, Med. Every course outline MUST conform to this framework. Keeps delivery consistent across tutors, allows students to move between products without re-onboarding, and makes outcomes measurable across the portfolio.

---

## The 5-stage delivery loop (same for every product)

```
1. Intake → 2. Onboard → 3. Deliver → 4. Measure → 5. Graduate (or Renew)
```

Every product ships against this loop. Only the *content* inside each stage varies.

---

## Stage 1 — Intake (Day 0)

**Goal:** qualify the student + capture baseline + signal premium experience from minute 1.

**Artefacts produced:**
- 5-min recorded assessment call (lead magnet from Section 13.6)
- Baseline CEFR level estimate (A1-C2)
- Product-specific diagnostic (see per-product outlines)
- Stated goal in student's own words
- Mental-health-informed intake flag (Rise layer) — any anxiety / stress signals in intake
- 24 hrs later: personalised 3-week plan email with tier recommendation

**Tools:**
- Calendly (assessment booking)
- Recording: Zoom Cloud OR Whisper local transcript
- Intake form: Typeform → Airtable
- Tier-match logic: simple decision tree based on level + goal + budget

**Pass gate:** student books their first coach session within 7 days of intake call, OR opts out cleanly.

---

## Stage 2 — Onboard (Day 1-7)

**Goal:** student hits their first "win" within 7 days of payment, so they stay.

**Universal onboarding actions:**
- Welcome WhatsApp audio (your voice) within 1 hr of payment
- Tutor introduction video (their assigned coach) within 24 hrs — name, 30-sec "why I teach"
- First session booked within 72 hrs
- Elevare AI practice companion provisioned (even for non-AI-standalone tiers, as included layer)
- Rise Day-1 confidence prompt sent (methodology layer)
- Expectation document: "What your 30 days will look like" — day-by-day preview

**First session protocol (same for all products):**
- 5 min: student talks about one thing they couldn't say last month that they want to say next month. (Captured as their personal North Star.)
- 15 min: tutor runs the product-specific diagnostic
- 15 min: first actual practice on student's North Star
- 5 min: tutor sends student home with one 10-min AI practice task + one coach-written "you can already do this" note (Rise layer win-spotting)

**Pass gate:** student completes session + uses Elevare AI once + returns on Day 3.

---

## Stage 3 — Deliver (Week 1-4 for AI/Speak/Pro/Med monthly; Week 1-12 for Fluency)

**Goal:** ship the learning experience consistently across tutors, measurably.

**Session cadence (standard):**

| Product | Live coach time | AI practice expected | Coach 1:1 check-in |
|---|---|---|---|
| Elevare AI | 0 (AI-only) | 15 min/day | — |
| Elevare Speak | 90 min/wk group (6-max cohort) | 10 min/day | 15-min bi-weekly |
| Elevare Fluency | 60 min/wk group + 30 min/wk 1:1 | 15 min/day | weekly |
| Elevare Pro | 60 min/wk 1:1 + 60 min/wk small group (3-max) | 15 min/day scenario-focused | weekly |
| Elevare Med | 60 min/wk 1:1 + OET mock every 2 weeks | 20 min/day vocab + scenarios | weekly |

**Session format (every session, every product):**
1. Warm-up (3 min) — Elevare AI warm-up mode
2. Review last session's homework (5 min) — coach confirms practice happened
3. Core teaching block (30-70 min depending on product)
4. Application (5-15 min) — student does the thing themselves in real-time
5. Debrief + next-week homework + Rise win-spotting (5 min)

**Tutor discipline (non-negotiable):**
- Notes logged in shared Google Sheet within 24 hrs
- Tagged skills practiced (feeds Elevare AI between-session recap prompts)
- Rise moment logged (what did the student do well that they didn't think they could?)
- Red flags escalated within 2 hrs (mental health, engagement drop, payment issues)

---

## Stage 4 — Measure (ongoing, formal at Day 15 + Day 30)

**Goal:** prove measurable progress matches the Elevare guarantee.

**What gets measured:**

| Metric | How | Cadence |
|---|---|---|
| CEFR-style conversational level | Coach assessment + AI role-play scoring | Intake + Day 15 + Day 30 |
| Minutes of AI practice logged | Auto-tracked via bot | Weekly |
| Tutor-rated skill progression | 5-skill rubric per product | Weekly |
| Student-reported confidence | 1-10 scale, one question: "How confident do you feel in [product-specific scenario]?" | Weekly |
| Product-specific milestone | See per-product outline | Varies |

**The Rise layer:** every measurement pairs an objective score with a "you have done X that you couldn't do on Day 1" specific observation. Progress is both measured AND felt.

**30-day decision point:** did the student hit the measurable progress threshold?
- YES → graduate criteria met, offer next-tier upsell
- PARTIAL → extend program free (per guarantee) + adjust plan
- NO → free extension + escalate to you for personal review

---

## Stage 5 — Graduate or Renew

**Graduate (for Fluency 12-week):** final assessment + public "graduation" (cohort celebration, certificate, LinkedIn badge). Students posting their graduation to LinkedIn is your best content.

**Renew (for monthly products Speak/Pro/Med):** auto-renewal via Whop, but at Day 25 coach sends personal "here's what we'd work on next month" message. Reduces passive churn.

**Upsell moment:** Day 30 is when students feel the progress. Offer the next tier:
- AI → Speak
- Speak → Pro or Fluency
- Fluency → Pro (if professional) or Med (if medical)
- Pro → Pro annual (at discount) or Pro + Med combo for medical professionals

**Win-back moment:** Day 45 for churned students — personal voice message from coach, not automation.

## Rise methodology — how the layer works inside every product

Rise is NOT a product or a session. It's a set of behaviours practiced by every tutor across every product:

1. **Win-spotting** — end every session by naming one specific thing the student did that they couldn't do before
2. **Safe-to-fail design** — role-play scenarios that are deliberately lower-stakes than the real situation (practice ordering coffee before the job interview)
3. **Mental-health awareness** — tutors trained to notice distress signals (avoidance, flat affect, catastrophising) and soft-escalate
4. **Reframing** — "you're stuck" becomes "you're at the edge of your current skill — that's where learning happens"
5. **Ownership transfer** — Week 1 coach drives, Week 4 student drives. Student leaves each program with self-practice routines, not dependence.

Every tutor completes a 90-min Rise methodology onboarding before taking students. Material lives at `courses/rise-tutor-training.md` (to be written).

## Tutor curriculum discipline

Per Section 16 agency model: tutors teach the Elevare curriculum, not their own.

**What tutors get:**
- Per-product lesson plans (Weeks 1-N)
- Script library for recurring scenarios
- Assessment rubrics
- Red-flag escalation runbook
- Rise methodology cheat sheet

**What tutors don't get:**
- Freedom to invent curriculum (breaks consistency)
- Direct commercial relationship with student (agency model)
- The right to use session recordings outside Elevare (IP assignment in contract)

**Who owns this:** Debby writes v1 of each product's curriculum. Tutors feedback weekly. Revisions monthly. After Month 3, senior tutor can help maintain under Debby's editorial sign-off.
