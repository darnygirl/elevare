# Elevare Speak — Course Outline & Implementation Plan

> Coach-led group cohort for conversation fluency. Conforms to `implementation-methodology.md` 5-stage loop. Full 4-week rolling curriculum.

---

## Positioning + ICP

**Primary ICP:** A2-B2 working adults globally who want conversation fluency in English. Not specialists — general fluency for daily life, work conversations, travel, social. Mid-tier of the funnel.

**Who Speak is NOT for:**
- Complete beginners (A0-A1) — they need a dedicated beginner track, not cohort
- Test prep students → Fluency (for IELTS/exam-focused) or Med (OET)
- Business-specialist needs → Pro
- Very advanced C1-C2 → they'll feel under-challenged, recommend 1:1 Pro instead

**Pricing:** $199/mo per Section 18. Format: 90-min group call weekly (6-max cohort) + 15-min bi-weekly 1:1 + Elevare AI daily practice + peer practice partner match.

**Cohort mechanics:** New cohort starts every 4 weeks. Students can join mid-cycle with a 30-min catch-up session. Cohort retention targets: 65% month-over-month (above industry average for consumer language learning).

## Stage 1 — Intake

**Assessment call (5-10 min):**

Diagnostic questions:
1. Why English? (work / travel / family / general confidence / other)
2. Current situations where English blocks you
3. Past learning experience — apps used, results
4. Time you can realistically commit (target: 30 min/day minimum)
5. Preferred style — structured curriculum vs free-form conversation?

**Diagnostic (5 min):**
- 2-min free conversation: "Tell me about your week so far in as much detail as you can"
- Score: fluency floor (does speech flow?), accuracy pattern (what grammar is slipping?), range (can they describe, compare, evaluate or only basic present-tense statements?)

**Tier-match output:**
- A2-B1 → Speak entry cohort (appropriate pace)
- B2 → Speak advanced cohort (more challenging topics, faster pace)
- Under A2 → deflect gently to self-study apps + "let's try again in 3 months"
- C1+ → Pro instead

**Welcome plan (within 24 hrs):**
- Cohort assignment + start date
- 3-week preview of what you'll practice
- First homework: 3 AI role-plays before first session

## Stage 3 — Deliver (4-week rolling curriculum)

### Week 1 — Small talk + introductions

**Learning objectives:**
- Introduce yourself clearly in 3 levels of formality
- Hold a 5-minute small-talk conversation without awkward silence
- Use active listening phrases to keep conversations going
- Describe what you do / where you're from / what you're interested in

**Core language blocks:**
- **Opening patterns** — 10 greetings/opening-lines across register (peer, stranger, senior, client)
- **Active listening markers** — "Oh really?" / "That's interesting" / "So what happened then?" — 15 variations to avoid repetition
- **Topic bridges** — "Speaking of which..." / "That reminds me..." / "On a related note..."
- **Graceful exits** — "Great talking to you" / "I should run, but let's catch up soon" — 5 register-appropriate variants

**Session plan (group, 90 min):**
- 10-min: cohort check-in, one sentence each on their week (low-pressure warmup)
- 15-min: teach active listening framework + demo
- 30-min: pair practice (rotate pairs every 10 min) — cohort members small-talk each other
- 15-min: teach topic bridges + graceful exits
- 15-min: 3-person groups, each runs a small-talk scenario with assigned exit condition
- 5-min: debrief, homework, Rise win-spotting

**Homework (between sessions):**
- 3× AI role-plays: meeting a neighbour / chatting at coffee shop / networking event
- 1× peer practice call (15 min) with rotating partner
- Real-world application: have one deliberate small-talk conversation in English this week, come back with the story

**Milestone:** by end of Week 1, student can hold 5-min small-talk with a stranger without the silence-and-nod default.

---

### Week 2 — Telling stories in English

**Learning objectives:**
- Tell a 2-min personal story with clear structure (past tense, narrative arc)
- Handle past-tense under real-time pressure (not just in writing)
- Use emotional language — not just factual — to make stories engaging
- Recover when you lose the thread mid-story

**Core language blocks:**
- **Narrative arc in English** — setup → complication → resolution
- **Past-tense fluency drills** — irregular verbs, past continuous vs simple, past perfect for background
- **Emotional language bank** — 25 words beyond "good/bad/nice" (stunned, grateful, frustrated, relieved, embarrassed)
- **Recovery phrases** — "Wait, let me back up..." / "Actually the important thing was..." / "Where was I? Oh yes..."

**Session plan (group, 90 min):**
- 10-min: warmup — each student tells one thing they did since last session
- 20-min: teach narrative arc + emotional language
- 30-min: story circle — each student tells a 2-min story on prompted theme. Peers + coach give 1 specific feedback each
- 15-min: drill past-tense + emotional-language swaps
- 10-min: pair practice — students tell each other the "same story twice" (once dry, once using new techniques)
- 5-min: debrief + homework + Rise win-spotting

**Homework:**
- 3× AI role-plays: tell a story about a memorable meal / a time you were embarrassed / a trip that went wrong
- Record yourself telling one real story (2-min). Transcribe. Circle past-tense slips.
- Peer practice call: each tells 1 new story

**Milestone:** by end of Week 2, student can tell a 2-min structured story with past-tense accuracy and one emotional-language phrase.

---

### Week 3 — Opinions + disagreement politely

**Learning objectives:**
- Express a strong opinion without sounding aggressive
- Disagree with someone politely while maintaining the relationship
- Use hedging language to soften claims
- Ask for clarification when you don't understand

**Core language blocks:**
- **Opinion-intro phrases** — "In my experience..." / "I tend to think..." / "From where I sit..." — 10 variations
- **Disagreement ladder** (soft to firm) — "I see what you mean, but..." / "I'd push back on that..." / "I actually think the opposite..."
- **Hedging language** — "generally speaking" / "in most cases" / "it depends a bit on..."
- **Clarification phrases** — "Sorry, could you repeat that last part?" / "When you say X, do you mean...?" — 10 variants to rotate

**Session plan (group, 90 min):**
- 10-min: warmup — agree/disagree with a controversial statement (ice cream is better than cake, etc.)
- 20-min: teach opinion + disagreement frameworks
- 30-min: structured debate — 3v3 on a relevant topic (remote work vs office, etc.) — 10-min debate + 20-min coaching breakdown
- 20-min: hedging drills — take strong claims and soften them 3 different ways each
- 10-min: clarification practice — coach speaks too fast / uses unfamiliar vocab, students ask for clarification
- 5-min: debrief + homework + Rise win-spotting

**Homework:**
- 3× AI role-plays: disagree with a friend about movie choice / push back on a work deadline / clarify a confusing instruction
- Peer practice: have one deliberately-opposing-views conversation
- Real-world: find one situation to politely disagree this week, report back

**Milestone:** by end of Week 3, student can disagree with a peer, maintain the relationship, and back up their position with one supporting reason.

---

### Week 4 — Dealing with unexpected situations (improv + recovery)

**Learning objectives:**
- Keep speaking when you don't know a word (circumlocution)
- Recover from a misunderstanding gracefully
- Handle unexpected questions without freezing
- Manage speaking under time pressure

**Core language blocks:**
- **Circumlocution** — describing a word you don't know (the thing you use to... / it's kind of like... / you know when you...)
- **Misunderstanding recovery** — "Let me try to say that again" / "I think I confused myself — what I mean is..."
- **Improv openers for any question** — "That's something I've thought about, actually..." / "Interesting — let me think for a second..."
- **Time-pressure phrases** — "Give me just a moment to gather my thoughts..." / "The short version is..."

**Session plan (group, 90 min):**
- 10-min: warmup — improv circle (one word each, building a story)
- 20-min: teach circumlocution + improv openers
- 30-min: improv exercises
  - "Describe this word without using the word" (each student gets 3 mystery words)
  - "Answer any question for 60 seconds" (coach fires random questions)
  - "Tell a story that must include these 3 random words"
- 20-min: misunderstanding recovery drills — coach deliberately misinterprets, students clarify
- 10-min: integration — each student fields 2 min of unpredictable questions from the group
- 5-min: debrief + cohort retro + homework + Rise win-spotting

**Homework:**
- 3× AI role-plays: describe an object you don't know the name of / recover from a misunderstanding / improvise for 60 sec
- Peer practice: "call me and tell me about something you've never talked about in English"
- Real-world: have one conversation this week where you had to improvise, report back

**Milestone:** by end of Week 4, student can circumlocute around unknown vocabulary, recover from misunderstandings, and answer unexpected questions without freezing.

## Post-Week 4 — Rolling programme (Months 2+)

The 4-week theme set repeats with new topics. Students don't repeat curriculum — they work through harder variants of the same frameworks.

**Month 2 themes:** the same 4 pillars applied to work contexts (small talk at meetings / telling stories in presentations / disagreeing in team settings / improv in interviews).

**Month 3 themes:** same 4 pillars applied to high-stakes social (meeting partner's family / small talk with senior executives / disagreeing with teachers / improvising in unfamiliar cultural contexts).

**After Month 3:** student customizes their focus. Either continue Speak (sometimes for years, as conversation maintenance) OR upgrade to Pro / Fluency / Med based on direction.

## Stage 4 — Measure

**Weekly:**
- Coach rubric (5-skill: fluency / accuracy / range / confidence / engagement)
- AI practice minutes + scenarios completed
- Peer practice partner completion (compliance signal)

**Monthly:**
- Student self-rating on target scenarios
- "Real-world win of the month" — coach asks students to report one successful English interaction they had outside the program

**Rise layer observation (weekly):**
- Coach logs 1 specific "you-couldn't-do-this-a-month-ago" observation per student
- Compiles to a monthly email: "4 things you can do now you couldn't 30 days ago" — retention magic

---

## Stage 5 — Graduate or Renew

**Auto-renewal default.** Speak is a long-term product for many — students can stay 6-12+ months as conversation maintenance.

**Natural graduation trigger:** student self-reports: "I'm not thinking about the frameworks anymore, I'm just talking." Coach offers either: (a) continue Speak as maintenance, (b) move to Pro for business-English specialization, (c) move to Fluency if committed to a measurable 12-week jump, (d) pause subscription with re-entry option.

**Churn prevention (Day 25 of each month):**
- Coach sends personal WhatsApp: "Here's what I'd work on with you next month if you continue..."
- Reduces passive churn dramatically vs silent auto-renewal

**Referral incentive:** $50 credit per new Speak student referred by a current student.

## Tutor training for Speak

Speak coaches need:
- **Group facilitation skills** (running a 6-person call, managing airtime, preventing one student from dominating)
- **Confidence-first approach** (Rise layer — find the win, not the mistake)
- **Patience with A2-B1 learners** (higher mistake density, slower pace)
- **Cultural bridge work** (most students have a non-Anglo cultural context, coach must honour that)

**Training module:** 90 minutes onboarding for new Speak coaches. Includes Rise methodology overview, recorded demo sessions to score, cohort management scenarios, red-flag escalation runbook.
