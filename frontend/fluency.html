The 12-week roadmap

Six phases from baseline to breakthrough

WEEKS 1-2

Fluency floor

Clear hesitation markers. Build automaticity on present-tense descriptions. Introduce yourself in 90 seconds without notes.

WEEKS 3-4

Range + confidence

Past + future tense under real-time pressure. Opinions with nuance. Compare, contrast, handle follow-up without freezing.

WEEKS 5-6

Real-world deployment

Apply frameworks to your specific upcoming scenarios. Build a personalized phrase bank for your deadline goal.

WEEKS 7-8

Precision + accuracy

Identify your 3-5 recurring grammar patterns. Target drills. Register matching — same message, different tones.

WEEKS 9-10

Complex communication

Nuance, sarcasm, implication. Storytelling with emotional arc. Persuasion techniques. Cultural English.

WEEKS 11-12

Integration + graduation

Weak-spot pass. Final assessment vs Week-0 baseline. Cohort graduation with certificate, LinkedIn badge, alumni community access.

Questions answered

FAQ

What's the difference between Fluency and Speak?

Speak is monthly, open-ended, general fluency. Fluency is a 12-week intensive with a structured arc, milestone assessments, and a graduation moment. Fluency is for people with a deadline — wedding, visa, move, interview. Speak is for ongoing improvement.

Do I need to already be at B1?

Yes — Fluency assumes intermediate baseline. The programme accelerates learners from B1 → B2+ (or B2 → C1+) in 12 weeks. Below B1 start with Speak.

What if I can't make every session?

One free catch-up session per programme. Beyond that we work around your schedule on the 1:1 side. Group sessions are recorded.

What's in the graduation package?

Certificate, LinkedIn badge, alumni community WhatsApp group, 30 days of post-course support. Many graduates move to Speak as ongoing maintenance.

How are the milestones measured?

5 dimensions: speaking fluency (words/min), grammar accuracy (slips per 100 words), listening comprehension (on native-pace content), writing (rubric-scored), confidence (self-report). You see your scores after every milestone.

When does the next cohort start?

New cohorts start every 4 weeks. Book the assessment to get the current cohort calendar.

Can I upgrade to Pro or Med after?

Yes — many Fluency graduates move to Pro (business English) or Med (medical English). Alumni get a discount on their next programme.
